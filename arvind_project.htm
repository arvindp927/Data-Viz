ABSTRACT

 

Collision data is used to understand why the collision occurs, to help identify high collision locations, to support the choice of safety programs or countermeasures and to assist the evaluations of countermeasure effectiveness. The main purpose of crash analysis is improve the safety by identifying collision patterns, mitigating collision severity and adopting suitable countermeasures.

The vehicle collision data analysis is used for safety improvements and better operation and maintenance of the transportation facilities. Historically many agencies used this data to identify top 10 highest vehicle collision locations and based on this significant budget was allocated towards mitigating those vehicle collision prone locations. It is also used for evaluation of safety of traffic control devices including traffic signal processing.

The purpose of this project is to explore and gain a better understanding of some of the factors that affect the likelihood of vehicle collision in Seattle. The results of this analysis allows us to proactively identify locations and prioritize safety improvements with the goal of preventing future crashes. The dataset consists of information of incident of collisions and the factors causing it. This information is used to extract insights by performing multivariate data analysis. The data is cleaned and new attributes are created as part of the data preprocessing. The visualization like diverging bar charts, multiple line plot, stacked bat chart, tree map, heat map, parallel co-ordinates, bubble chart, moving average and geographical plots are created to present the hidden insights from the dataset.

 

INTRODUCTION

 

Collisions are rare events. A typical intersection experiences less than one collision per million vehicles that navigate the intersection. Collisions occur because of various factors including the driver, vehicle, roadway, and environment.

The Seattle Department of Transportation (SDOT) collects and maintains volume data for vehicles (including trucks), pedestrians, and bicycles. This data is used by engineers and planners to select future project locations, support grant applications, and track the performance of traffic projects once they are installed. SDOT has recently started regularly collecting vehicle speed data. Speed data is particularly useful for making traffic safety decisions such as those connected with traffic calming, Safe Routes to School, and crossing improvements.

Most of the collisions result from road users inattention, collision data helps to gauge the effectiveness of engineering and enforcement efforts and can also assists in identifying locations that require additional engineering treatments or enhancements. There were about 12471 incidents recorded in the 2017. SDOT partners with the Seattle police department (SPD) to help focusing on enforcement in areas of concern for traffic safety. Most of the collision report showed significant reduction in the collision over the years
